INTRODUCTION
================================================================================

Immunocytochemistry is a technique that uses antibodies to detect antigens in
cells. Here we describe the basic steps for fixing and labeling cells in culture
with a primary antibody against a target protein and a fluorescent secondary
antibody. This protocol outlines the steps for fixing and labeling HeLa cells for
a target protein using the formaldehyde fixation method. The protocol may need to
be optimized for different cells, target proteins, etc.

PROCEDURE
================================================================================

Section 1: Seeding cells

1. Place a sterile poly-D-lysine coated coverslip in each well of a 24-well cell
   culture treated plate.

2. Seed 5 x 10Â³ HeLa cells per well.

3. Allow the HeLa cells to grow to the desired density before labeling.

Section 2: Fixing and permeabilizing cells

1. Gently aspirate the media from the 24-well plate.

2. Wash each well with 500 ÂµL of PBS, remove the wash, and dispose of it in an
   appropriate waste container.

3. Fix each well with 500 ÂµL of cold 4% paraformaldehyde in PBS on ice for 15
   min.

   proteins, it may not be the best fixation method for all. Alternative fixation

4. Remove the paraformaldehyde and follow your institution's laboratory safety
   guidelines for disposing of waste in the appropriate container.

5. Wash 3x for 5 min in 500 ÂµL PBS on a rocking platform.

6. Permeabilize cells for 10 min at room temperature (RT) on a rocking platform

7. Remove the permeabilization buffer and dispose of it in an appropriate waste

8. Wash 3x for 5 min in 500 ÂµL PBS on a rocking platform.

16. Add 1 drop of anti-fade mounting medium to the microscope slide.

17. Gently place the coverslip on the microscope slide with the cell side facing

18. Observe the cell labeling on a microscope with appropriate fluorescent
