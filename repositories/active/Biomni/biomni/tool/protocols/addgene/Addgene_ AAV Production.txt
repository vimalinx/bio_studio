REAGENT PREPARATION
================================================================================

DMEM Complete: 10% v/v FBS and 4 mM L-alanyl-L-glutamine (or stable alternative,
such as glutaGRO)

To a 500 mL bottle of DMEM high glucose, add 55 mL of heat-inactivated FBS and 5
mL of glutaGRO 11 mL of 200 mM L-alanyl-L-glutamine. Store at 4 Â°C.

D1 + 0.1 M sorbitol (optional): DMEM low glucose + 1% v/v HI-FBS + 0.1 M sorbitol

In our hands, we have found that a lower glucose, lower FBS media causes less
filter clogging during harvesting. The addition of sorbitol has been shown to
increase viral titers by up to 1.8-fold (Rego et al., 2018).

To prepare D1 + 0.1 M sorbitol, add 57 mL of 1.46 M sorbitol, 7.5 mL HI-FBS, and
7.5 mL 100X glutaGRO, 7.5 mL of 7.5% Sodium Bicarbonate, 7.5 mL 1 M HEPES to 750
mL DMEM + 1 g/L glucose.

1 mg/mL polyethylenimine (PEI) solution:

1. Dissolve 100 mg of PEI powder into 100 mL of deionized water.

2. While stirring, slowly add hydrochloric acid until the solution clears.

3. Check the pH of the solution and use hydrochloric acid or sodium hydroxide to
   adjust the pH to 7.0.

   ðŸ’¡ PRO TIP: The pH of this solution will drift pretty rapidly upon addition
   of acid or base. Add only a few drops at a time. Allow them to mix and recheck
   the pH to prevent over or undershooting the desired pH.

4. Allow the solution to mix for 10 min and then recheck the pH to ensure that it
   has not drifted.

5. Filter the solution through a 0.22 Î¼m membrane.

6. Aliquot 500â€“1000 Î¼L into sterile tubes.

7. Store the tubes at -80 Â°C.

8. After thawing, the solution can be stored at 4 Â°C for up to 2 months. After 2
   months, discard the tube and thaw a new working stock.

40% POLYETHYLENE GLYCOL (PEG) 8000 solution:

1. Dissolve 400 g of PEG 8000 and 24 g of NaCl into deionized water and adjust to
   a final volume of 1000 mL.

2. Stir at RT until fully dissolved.

   ðŸ’¡ PRO TIP: This step is challenging due to the high viscosity of PEG.
   Stirring under medium heat will promote faster dissolution.

3. Adjust the pH to ~7.4.

4. Autoclave or sterile filter.

   ðŸ’¡ PRO TIP: Stirring during the cooling period is recommended or the solution
   may separate into phases.

5. Aliquot and store at 4 Â°C.

PROCEDURE
================================================================================

1. Trypsinize and resuspend the HEK293T cells from 2 x T-175 flasks. Cells should
   be at ~80% confluence. For each T-175 flask:
   â€¢ Aspirate culture media and rinse once with 10 mL of PBS.
   â€¢ Aspirate PBS and add 2 mL of 0.05% Trypsin/EDTA. Wait ~2 min.
   â€¢ Neutralize trypsin by adding 10 mL of DMEM Complete.
   â€¢ Pipet back and forth vigorously multiple times to obtain a single cell
     suspension (no clumps of cells).

2. Pool cells from 2 x T-175 flasks. Adjust volume to 300 mL with DMEM complete
   media and mix.

3. Seed all cells in 1 CS2. Return to incubator for 48 h.

4. Trypsinize and resuspend cells from the CS2. Cells should be at ~80%
   confluence.
   â€¢ Aspirate culture media and rinse once with 60 mL PBS.
   â€¢ Aspirate PBS and add 35 mL of 0.05% Trypsin/EDTA.
   â€¢ Wait ~2-3 minutes for cells to detach.
   â€¢ Gently tap the sides of the CS2 to help detach the cells, add 200 mL of DMEM
     complete media, then transfer the cells into a sterile bottle.
   â€¢ Rinse the CS2 with 100 mL of DMEM complete medium and pool with the cells
     harvested in the previous step.
   â€¢ Pipette back and forth or shake vigorously to obtain a single cell suspension
     (no clumps).

5. Count cells using a hemocytometer or cell counter.

6. Seed 350 million cells from the CS2 into one CS5 with a total volume of 850 mL.
   Return to the incubator for 24 hâ€“36 h.

7. Proceed with transfection:
   â€¢ Calculate the amount of each plasmid needed to have a 1:1:1 molar ratio with 2
     mg total DNA per CS5

   â€¢ Add 4 mL of PEI (1:2 Î¼g DNA to Î¼g PEI ratio). Shake the bottle up/down

    â€¢ Distribute the media evenly between the layers.
    â€¢ Return the CS5 to the incubator for ~72 h.

13. Transfer cells and media into 500 mL conical vessels.

14. Rinse CS5 once with 100 mL of PBS and add to the harvested media and cells.

15. Centrifuge at 3900 rpm for 20 min at 4 Â°C to pellet the cells.
