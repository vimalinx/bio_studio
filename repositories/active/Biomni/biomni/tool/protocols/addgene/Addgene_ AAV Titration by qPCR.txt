PROTOCOL: AAV Titration by qPCR
SOURCE: https://www.addgene.org/protocols/aav-titration-qpcr-using-sybr-green-technology/
================================================================================

INTRODUCTION
================================================================================

The AAV Titration protocol can be used to determine the number of
genome-containing particles of an AAV prep using SYBR green technology.

This protocol was validated using an internal reference AAV of known titer,
100837-AAV1, and by measuring the titer of samples obtained from academic viral
vector cores. Our titers were similar to those reported by these institutions.

This protocol is for a 96-well plate with 20 ŒºL reaction volume.

Last Update: February 13, 2019

Estimate of time required: ~3 h


WORKFLOW TIMELINE
================================================================================

Plate set-up: 2 h

qPCR run: 1.5 h

Data analysis: 30 min

GENERAL CONSIDERATIONS
================================================================================

‚Ä¢ Always run standards and samples in duplicate at least

‚Ä¢ If possible, include an AAV reference sample of known titer. The reference
  material should have a titer within 1-log of the expected titer of the samples
  being tested.

‚Ä¢ Always include a No Template Control (NTC), i.e master mix + water

‚Ä¢ Whenever possible use a multichannel pipet to minimize pipetting error and
  variability

‚Ä¢ Mix samples very well by pipetting back and forth multiple times at each step


REAGENT PREPARATION
================================================================================

Master Mix:

Count the number of samples (n) and prepare master mix for an additional 10
samples (n+10 ‚Äì the additional amount will ensure that there is enough master mix
for all samples). Each sample requires 15 ŒºL of master mix.

üí° PRO TIPS:

‚Ä¢ Use a "Universal" SYBR master mix which contains a high-quality DNA polymerase
  and a blend of dTTP/dUTP to minimize carryover contamination. The master mix
  should also contain an internal passive reference (typically ROX dye), to
  normalize non-PCR‚Äìrelated fluorescence fluctuations and to minimize well-to-well
  variability that result from a variety of causes, such as pipetting error and
  sample evaporation.

‚Ä¢ Make the master mix after all the samples have been added to the qPCR plate.
  Start by adding water, then SYBR master mix, then the forward and reverse
  primers. Vortex briefly, immediately before use.

‚Ä¢ Use a reservoir and a multichannel pipette to dispense the master mix into the
  wells.

Master Mix Recipe:

Reagent                    | Amount for ONE Reaction | Amount for 100 reactions
---------------------------|------------------------|--------------------------
Universal SYBR Master Mix  | 10 ŒºL                  | 1,000 ŒºL
2X                         |                        |
100 ŒºM Forward Primer      | 0.15 ŒºL                | 15 ŒºL
100 ŒºM Reverse Primer      | 0.15 ŒºL                | 15 ŒºL
Nuclease Free Water        | 4.7 ŒºL                 | 470 ŒºL


PROCEDURE
================================================================================

1. Prepare a plasmid stock of 2 x 10‚Åπ molecules/ŒºL to generate a standard curve:

   One option is to use plasmid #59462 from Addgene. The values highlighted below
   in red were calculated using this plasmid, but will change if you use a
   different plasmid.

   Sample Calculation:
   ‚Ä¢ Size in bp of Addgene plasmid #59462: 6208 bp
   ‚Ä¢ Concentration of Addgene plasmid #59462: 1.07 Œºg/ŒºL
   ‚Ä¢ Molecular Weight: 6208 bp √ó 650 dalton/bp = 4.03 √ó 10‚Å∂ g/mole
   ‚Ä¢ Moles/Œºl: 1.07 Œºg/ŒºL √ó 1 g/10‚Å∂ Œºg √ó 1 / 4.03 √ó 10‚Å∂ g/mole = 2.65 √ó 10‚Åª¬π¬≥
     moles/ŒºL
   ‚Ä¢ Molecules/Œºl: 2.65 √ó 10‚Åª¬π¬≥ moles/ŒºL √ó 6.022145 √ó 10¬≤¬≥ molecules/mole = 1.59
     √ó 10¬π¬π molecules/ŒºL
   ‚Ä¢ To obtain a solution at 2 √ó 10‚Åπ molecules/ŒºL: 1.59 √ó 10¬π¬π / 2 √ó 10‚Åπ = 79.8X
     dilution
   ‚Ä¢ Therefore we need to dilute 1.25 ŒºL stock into 98.74 ŒºL H‚ÇÇO

   üí° PRO TIP: Once a validated standard curve is obtained, make a small aliquot
   of each standard (enough for 1 or 2 plates) and store at -20 ¬∞C. Once a
   standard is thawed do not freeze it again but store at 4 ¬∞C and use within 1
   week.

   Keep track of the Ct value for each standard over time. They should remain
   within 0.5 Ct of their initial value. If the Ct value of the standard starts
   to drift, it's time to make a new one.

   When developing the assay multiple plasmids containing ITR were tested.
   Plasmid #59462 is one plasmid that gave reliable and consistent results. Use
   the recommended plasmid, or test multiple plasmids to find a suitable one.

   Some labs have reported better results when the plasmid is linearized.

2. Treat the purified AAV samples with DNase I to eliminate any contaminating
   plasmid DNA carried over from the production process (DNase does not penetrate
   the virion).

   ‚Ä¢ 5 ŒºL sample + 39 ŒºL H‚ÇÇO + 5 ŒºL 10X DNase buffer + 1 ŒºL DNase
   ‚Ä¢ Gently mix sample (do not vortex)
   ‚Ä¢ Incubate 30 min at 37 ¬∞C
   ‚Ä¢ Transfer to ice

   ** Critical: do NOT treat your plasmid standard with DNase **

3. Make 6 serial dilutions, in duplicate, of your standard curve plasmid (2 √ó
   10‚Åπ stock made in step #1):

   Volume of 2 √ó 10‚Åπ stock | Volume of nuclease-free | Molecules per ŒºL
   or previous dilution   | water (uL)              |
   (ŒºL)                    |                         |
   ------------------------|-------------------------|------------------
   10                      | 90                      | 2 √ó 10‚Å∏
   10 of 2 √ó 10‚Å∏ dilution  | 90                      | 2 √ó 10‚Å∑
   10 of 2 √ó 10‚Å∑ dilution  | 90                      | 2 √ó 10‚Å∂
   10 of 2 √ó 10‚Å∂ dilution  | 90                      | 2 √ó 10‚Åµ
   10 of 2 √ó 10‚Åµ dilution  | 90                      | 2 √ó 10‚Å¥
   10 of 2 √ó 10‚Å¥ dilution  | 90                      | 2 √ó 10¬≥

   üí° PRO TIP: To help stabilize the standards add carrier DNA to a final
   concentration of 4 ug/mL to each standard dilution.

4. Dilute DNase-treated and AAV reference samples according to the dilution
   scheme in the table below:

   Dilution Series | Volume of sample | Volume of nuclease | Dilution | Total
                   | (uL)             | free water (uL)    | factor   | dilution
   ----------------|------------------|--------------------|----------|----------
   Dilution 1      | 5 uL AAV stock   | 45 uL              | 10X      | 10X
   (DNase step)    |                  |                    |          |
   Dilution 2      | 5 uL Dil. 1     | 95 uL              | 20X      | 200X
   Dilution 3      | 20 uL Dil. 2    | 80 uL              | 5X       | 1000X
   Dilution 4      | 20 uL Dil. 3    | 80 uL              | 5X       | 5000X
   Dilution 5      | 20 uL Dil. 4    | 80 uL              | 5X       | 25000X
   Dilution 6      | 20 uL Dil. 5    | 80 uL              | 5X       | 125000X
   Dilution 7      | 20 uL Dil. 6    | 80 uL              | 5X       | 625000X
   Dilution 8      | 20 uL Dil. 7    | 80 uL              | 5X       | 3125000X

   Dilutions highlighted in green are the ones loaded onto the qPCR plate for
   most samples.

   ‚Ä¢ If sample is expected to have a titer <1 √ó 10¬π¬≤ GC/mL, use dilutions 3‚Äì6
   ‚Ä¢ If sample is expected to have a titer >3 √ó 10¬π¬≥ GC/mL, use dilutions 5‚Äì8

   Note: at Addgene, samples typically range from 1 √ó 10¬π¬≤ GC/mL to >2 √ó 10¬π¬≥
   GC/mL and we use an internal reference virus that is 1 √ó 10¬π¬≥ GC/mL. We
   recommend always using a reference within 1-log of the expected titer.

   üí° PRO TIPS:
   ‚Ä¢ The quality of the sample dilution series is critical. Make sure to pipet
     each dilution up and down at least 10 times, and use at least half of the
     final volume (mix with >50 uL if your well contains 100 uL)
   ‚Ä¢ Use a multichannel pipette to load the standards and samples onto the qPCR
     plate

5. Set up and load the 96-well plate:
   ‚Ä¢ Load 5 ŒºL of each standard in duplicate
   ‚Ä¢ Load 5 ŒºL of each sample in duplicate. Do not forget to include a no
     template control (NTC = master mix + water).
   ‚Ä¢ Add 15 ŒºL of Master Mix per well and mix well by pipetting back and forth
     at least 5 times.
   ‚Ä¢ Seal plate with transparent film.
   ‚Ä¢ Centrifuge at 3,000 rpm for 2 min to bring the sample to the bottom of the
     tube.

6. Run the following protocol in your qPCR instrument using SYBR detection:

   98 ¬∞C 3 min / 98 ¬∞C 15 sec / 58 ¬∞C 30 sec / read plate/ repeat 39x from step
   3 / melt curve

7. Perform data analysis using the instrument's software. Determine the physical
   titer of samples (viral genomes (vg)/mL) based on the standard curve and the
   sample dilutions.

   üí° PRO TIP: Make sure that the qPCR is valid by checking to the following:
   ‚Ä¢ Standard curve: R¬≤ (coefficient of correlation) ~ 1.0, E (efficiency of PCR)
     ~100% (90%‚Äì110% range is acceptable)
   ‚Ä¢ Baseline removal: all samples will have some small amount of background
     signal that is most evident during initial PCR cycles. This background
     signal must be removed to accurately determine differences between samples.
   ‚Ä¢ Melt curve analysis: a single peak should be seen. The presence of a second
     peak at a temperature of ~70‚Äì75 ¬∞C usually indicates the presence of primer
     dimers which can increase background signal and alter the Ct values of your
     samples.
   ‚Ä¢ Quality of your standard curve: you should observe differences in Ct values
     that make sense for your dilutions (~3.3 difference Ct for a 10-fold dilution
     is appropriate).
   ‚Ä¢ Quality of duplicates: Exclude duplicates from analyses if there is more than
     a 0.5 Ct difference between them.
